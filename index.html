A Logic Gate is an abstraction on top of a digital circuit. A Logic Gate acts as a type of switch. There are many types of switches in the world of Digital Circuits: Mechanical Switches, used in early computers; Electromechanical Switches were used in telephone switching systems; Electronic Switches (Semiconductor-Based) lay the foundation of modern logic gates, of which there many as well: There are transistors, diodes, optical switches, and even newer still are the Quantum and Spintronic Switches that operate on spin states. The type of switch demonstrated here is an disjunctive switch also called an OR Gate: This means the gate is closed when either one of the inputs are "on", or when both are "on". Otherwise the switch (circuit) is open and no current is being allowed through; in particular when both are "off". Logic Gates are closely related to a branch of mathematics called Boolean Algebra. Other types of Logic Gates include: AND Gate, XOR Gate, NAND Gate etc.
